Core Modules

Powerful Features for Student Collaboration

PeerLink is engineered to solve student matching friction and facility underutilization by providing standard tools for information technology and computer science departments.

🎯

Bilateral Cosine Similarity Matcher

Leverages content-based vector calculations to compare subject enrollments, bilateral skill exchange needs, and availability. Recommends highly compatible peers.

  • ✓ Subject syllabus matching
  • ✓ Reciprocal teach-learn alignment
  • ✓ Real-time weight configuration
📅

Visual Scheduler & Synchronization

A high-fidelity time selection matrix helps students map vacant slots. It automatically identifies and lists overlapping hours to schedule session slots.

  • ✓ Drag-to-select interface
  • ✓ Auto-calculates common hours
  • ✓ Prevents scheduling overlaps
🎥

Integrated Video Call & Chat Workspace

Direct video calling inside the browser lets students meet without switching apps. Supports chat messages and screen sharing controls.

  • ✓ Webcam video and audio
  • ✓ Built-in messaging chat room
  • ✓ Simulated screen share modes
User Flow

How PeerLink Connects Students

Establishing study collaborations has never been simpler. Follow these 4 easy steps to start.

1

Register & Define Profile

Sign up with your 12-digit LRN. Tell us about your subjects and what you can help others with.

2

Set Availability Schedule

Map out your study schedule availability in our visual selector. The system uses this to verify matching free time.

3

Tune Recommendation Weights

Go to the matches playground tab and drag the priority sliders. Candidates sort instantly in real time using Cosine Similarity.

4

Send Invite & Collaborate

Send a connection invite to your match. Once accepted, you can chat, schedule virtual meetings, and call via video.

About PeerLink

PeerLink is an AI-Based Student Collaboration System for Secondary Education — a Smart Study Partner Finder with Subject, Skills, and Schedule-Based Recommendation System specifically designed for Junior High School (JHS) and Senior High School (SHS) students under the DepEd K-12 curriculum.

By integrating a multi-dimensional recommendation engine based on Cosine Similarity vectors, this system matches students based on subject overlap, bilateral skill exchanges, and schedule availability. This helps secondary students find the right study partner, reduce academic isolation, and improve peer collaboration across all tracks and strands.
